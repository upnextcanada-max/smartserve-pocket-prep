Question 1 SmartServe

What is the legal drinking age in Ontario?

Cram Sheet

Study before the proctored test only. This is not a live-test answer key.

Tap Auto-Run Board to cycle through every answer pattern.

Legal Ages

Serve/sell/handle/deliver at 18 if properly trained. Buy, possess, accept delivery, or consume at 19.

ID Trigger

Check ID when someone looks under 25 or acts unusual. If doubt remains, refuse.

ID Rules

Valid government photo ID with DOB. Never expired ID. Never student cards. Do not ask for Health Card; secondary only if offered.

Health Card

Do not ask for an Ontario Health Card. If voluntarily offered, it can only be used as secondary ID and accepted at your discretion.

Tampered ID

Reject ID with altered dates, dull/faded colours, mismatched fonts, cuts, bubbles, peeling laminate, uneven edges, or photo/description mismatch.

BAC Chart

Uses sex, weight, standard drinks, and time. Estimate only. BAC can rise after the last drink.

Lowest BAC

Same standard drinks: lowest BAC is usually the person with higher body weight, more time since drinking, and male sex on the BAC chart.

Standard Drinks

0.6 oz / 17 mL pure alcohol. Beer/cider/RTD 12 oz at 5%; wine 5 oz at 12%; spirits 1.5 oz at 40%.

Formula

60 divided by ABV percent = ounces for one standard drink. Example: 20% port = 3 oz.

BAC Colours

Green 0.00-0.049. Yellow 0.05-0.079 warn range. Red 0.08+ criminal driving threshold.

Only Time

Food, water, coffee, showers, or sleepiness do not instantly lower BAC. Only time lowers BAC.

High Tolerance

Tolerance hides signs, not BAC. A tolerant guest can still be impaired and unsafe to serve.

Signs

Slurred/loud speech, poor coordination, glassy eyes, slow reactions, confusion, drowsiness, mood swings, aggression.

Refuse Service

Refuse minors, invalid ID, intoxicated/apparently intoxicated guests, unsafe conduct, illegal hours, or delivery to an intoxicated person.

Eject / Remove

Manage or remove guests who are intoxicated, violent, quarrelsome, disorderly, unlawful, or creating safety risk.

Deny Entry

Deny entry to intoxicated people, barred/ejected same-day guests, people causing disorder or safety risk, and anyone over legal capacity.

Over-Service

Count drinks, slow service, offer food/water, communicate with staff, stop service early, document, arrange safe ride.

Civil Liability

Can arise from over-serving, serving minors/intoxicated guests, unsafe premises, or harm after the guest leaves.

LLCA

Ontario rules for licensing, sale, service, delivery, possession, consumption, inspections, enforcement, and penalties.

AGCO Risk

Minors, intoxicated customers, outside hours, illegal activity, unsafe premises, blocked inspections, or licence-condition breaches.

AGCO Suspension

The classic answer is serving/selling to anyone under 19. Also high-risk: intoxicated service, illegal hours, unsafe conduct, or ignoring conditions.

Underage Penalties

Serving or selling to a minor can mean AGCO/police penalties, fines, licence suspension or revocation, employer liability, and personal responsibility.

Retail Hours

Grocery/convenience retail sale and pickup: 7 a.m. to 11 p.m. Delivery: 9 a.m. to 11 p.m.

Retail Products

Licensed grocery/convenience stores can sell beer, cider, wine, and RTDs. Spirits may not be sold.

Convenience Licence

Convenience store: 4,000 sq ft or less, at least half retail floor space in food, at least five food categories, not primarily a pharmacy.

Penalty Numbers

Selling to minor: up to $100,000. Selling to intoxicated person or outside hours: up to $50,000.

Sandy's Law

FASD/pregnancy warning sign must be prominent in licensed establishments and at least 8 x 10 inches.

SOP Private Event

Private SOP: invited guests only, no public advertising, no profit from alcohol sales, no drinking contests, and no service to anyone under 19.

Service Hours

Licensed establishments usually 9 a.m. to 2 a.m.; New Year's Eve to 3 a.m.; clear service signs within 45 minutes.

Scenario Instinct

Pick the safest lawful action: check ID, refuse unsafe service, get backup, document, and arrange transportation.
